Speculative Salon 1.0 is a process-based project which uses machine learning to create artworks as a form of speculative collaboration based on the artistic work of 70 female artists who lived between the 15th and 20th centuries. They are generated for the duration of the exhibition 'Needles in a Haystack', currently on display at The Palace - National Art Gallery in Sofia, and only during the museum's opening hours. The image is displayed for one minute and then preserved as a dynamic online archive. You can read more about it here and in Bulgarian here. It is a project by Natalia Jordanova and developed by David Rau. For images of the show, check Instagram, and for special requests, send an email to nataliqiordanova@gmail.com.
